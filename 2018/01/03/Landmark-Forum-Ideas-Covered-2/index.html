Landmark Forum Ideas Covered – Part 2

Awareness of the Filter.

Awareness of the story as the filter. Otherwise Known as making stuff up.

II. Already Always Listening™
In the Already Always Listening segment, we visit the notion that while we think of ourselves as open-minded and objective, in fact our approach to ourselves, our circumstances, and others is often filtered and even obscured by pre-existing notions and ideas – by our upbringing, our values, our past experiences.
In this session, we see those filters for what they are—an all-pervasive influence that profoundly colors our relationships with people, circumstances, and even ourselves. An awareness of these filters, and a recognition of the striking limits that they impose, allows for a refreshing freedom. People, situations, and our approach to life alter dramatically.^1

Reading the first topic title, Already Always Listening™, what is it referring to?

Again, the concepts in Landmark Forum, as they are shared and explained to participants, will probably have little or nothing to do with how I take liberties unfolding them. If for some reason there are parallels, it will most likely be to the connection the concepts have to other traditions and disciplines, which Landmark has freely borrowed from.

So what is Already Always Listening™ pointing to? I’m not sure but there’s a lot to chew on. It hints at how we ought to always be ready to pay attention. It doesn’t take much to conclude that it’s something most of us have a hard time with. Then there is that filtering mentioned. Why then do we find it so difficult to pay attention to what develops in front of us, without filtering?

We don’t relate to what’s in front of us because we quickly develop a story, the filter mentioned. The filter is the story. It clouds and twists what’s in front of us. And stories must be fed the fuel that creates them.

The details of the story do not really matter so it’s no use trying to get to the bottom of it. It’s enough to know the story obscures what is occurring. The story becomes a distraction from what requires our attention.

Plenty has been written on filtering. Nowadays talk about ‘being in the moment,’ about ‘being in the now,’ is everywhere, so it seems. It’s almost gotten to the point that mere mention of ‘be in the present moment’ takes us away from it. This is the concern with anything profound yet ordinary as it becomes part of the vernacular. This should be good, but personally speaking, it takes work getting at the heart of this being present thing. It’s a curious kind of work. Curious because the work has to do with learning to relax, feeling ourselves settle. But back to the filter.

Ways that describe this filtering will make more sense than others. What resonates with me is reminding myself that there is little or nothing I know about any given situation. Each situation encountered presents us with much we are in the dark about. I mean, each situation bombards us with fresh sensory information we’ve never encountered before. We wander off with some story and a dull fog sets. We take a break from the effort of paying attention. We become lazy, arrogant, and space off.

This is pretty much what that ‘filter’ amounts to. But we rally just tune off and go to sleep. At some level we believe it’s all too familiar to us and our attention is not required.

Reminding ourselves that ‘this is fresh, new,’ and, in some sense, that we really don’t know what’s going on, is helpful. I know, it sounds odd. How, after all, can any situation be familiar and tainted with what we don’t know, can’t know, nor anticipate? I think the story, the narration, we spin is the arrogance part. We don’t wish to admit we’re in the dark about any situation and we start just making shit up. Dropping this arrogance takes work, it takes effort. But a simple and kindly reminder to ourselves such as, “There you go again, making shit up,” can help us drop the filter and return to engaging.

The freedom mentioned is discovered when we do this. If awareness of the filtering is present, there is no judgment taking place via some story, then plenty of room to move around should open up.

Test it with the next conversation you have with someone. It will become especially clear if your interest in the person and what they are sharing with you is of little interest to you. It should not take but a few seconds to see your mind wandering off into some story, most likely having nothing to do with the person or the conversation. Noting this is important, it’s the ‘awareness of these filters’ mentioned. But increasingly noticing, staying more and more with the noticing, takes work at developing.

There are many ways to develop our ability to remain with any situation. Training involves making the effort to stay with a conversation. But there are more formal ways to develop this in us that have been tried and tested for hundreds of years. I have no idea what Landmark Forum does to help people develop this awareness of filtering, but there are many ways to develop it that will be as effective, if not more.
